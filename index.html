There have been several questions about the Assignment deadlines. Please keep in mind that this course does not come with a Statement of Accomplishment or a Certificate, so for practical purposes, there really are no “official” deadlines. That said, it is helpful if the majority of students maintain a somewhat similar schedule because it facilitates discussion in the forums. In an attempt to allow flexibility but also maintain a fixed timeline we have decided to allow 15 days for all Assignments and Exams. We settled on 15 because it allows for 2 weeks, including two full weekends, which is helpful to students who are working during the week.

Two days!


We're just two days away from the launch of Statistic One. I’m thrilled to report that we now have approximately 100,000 students!

Expect to receive a message from me each week with a preview of what's to come. The first of these messages will be delivered Saturday at 12:01am EDT.

I’ve said it before and I’ll say it again, learning statistics is like learning a new language. The acquisition of any new language is challenging and sometimes very frustrating. The key to success is to immerse yourself in this new world of statistics. Try to engage with the course material frequently. For example, it is better to spend 1 hour per day 5 days a week on the material than to do everything in 5 hours on 1 day. Practice, practice, practice! And patience.

One of the goals of Statistics One is to get as many people in the world possible to speak the language of statistics. Please help me achieve this goal by staying in the course to completion. With 100,000 students, this is one of the largest online courses ever offered. Statistically speaking, if retention is strong then this could become the largest group to complete a course, not just enroll. Let’s do it!

That’s all for now. Have a great weekend.

Welcome to Statistics One!

Thank you for joining Statistics One! The purpose of this message is to remind you that the course officially begins one week from today. You can now review the final syllabus, an introduction video, and other materials here. Also, I will send weekly email announcements summarizing the tasks and goals for the week.

You should plan to allocate between 4 and 8 hours per week to Statistics One. Each week there will be two lectures, a lab, and a data analysis assignment. The lectures are less than one hour running time but you may need extra time to pause, rewind, and/or take notes. The weekly lab will be a bit shorter and should take no more than 30 minutes. Finally, the data analysis assignments will take at least one hour to complete and could take up to three hours (for example, if you are new to data analysis).

Learning statistics is, in many ways, like learning a new language. To become fluent you must practice, practice, practice. The best approach to the material is to devote an hour or so per day to the course. This will allow for frequent, repeated practice, which promotes learning and memory.

That’s all for now. I will send another email message on September 20th, two days before the course begins. For now, enjoy the end of summer (or winter, wherever you are!).

Sincerely,
